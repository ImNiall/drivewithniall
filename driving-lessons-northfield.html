Support from beginner to test ready

Complete beginners can start with controls, cockpit drill, moving off, stopping and basic road positioning. Learners with experience can work on the areas that are holding them back, such as roundabouts, manoeuvres, independent driving, complex junctions, meeting traffic or mock-test style driving.

If you are close to your practical test, we can use lessons to identify repeat faults and build a clearer plan. That might include improving observations, choosing safer speeds, planning earlier, staying calm after mistakes and understanding how a drive is assessed.

Northfield learners often benefit from practising how to stay organised when several things happen at once: pedestrians near crossings, traffic waiting to turn, parked cars narrowing the road, buses stopping, or drivers approaching quickly from behind. Instead of just telling you to “look more,” lessons focus on what to look for, when to look, and how to make the car match what you have seen.
